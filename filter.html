Filters.


Filters could be found by a number of names on a synthesiser such as, “VCF”, “HPF”, “LPF”, “BPF” or simply “Filter”. Filters are used to shape the waveforms by blocking certain frequencies, while allowing other frequencies to be heard. Filters play a big part in giving a synthesizer it’s own unique character. There are various types of filters which behave differently.


Below are buttons labeled “Low Pass” (AKA High Cut), “High Pass” (AKA Low Cut), and “Band Pass”. These refer to the different types of filters one can apply to shape the sound. If you press the play button you will hear white noise (synthesisers often have a noise source), and see the frequency spectrum illustrated by the frequency analyzer. There is also the choice to flip from white noise to some waveforms we saw on the previous page. Have a play around with the slider labeled “Cutoff”, and have a flip through the different filter types starting with the white noise selected, then hearing the other waveforms.


Caution!

Audio could be loud. Make sure to set your volume appropriately.



You can clearly hear and see that the filter is removing the frequencies selected by the ‘cut off’ slider in a smooth sweep motion. The low pass filter will cut out frequencies from high too low. The high pass filter does the opposite and cuts out frequencies from low too high. The band pass filter allows us to select frequencies between high and low.


The ‘Resonance’ (often found as ‘Res’) slider amplifies the cutoff frequency. Have a play around with the ‘Resonance’ slider in combination with the ‘Cutoff’ slider. There is also an LFO routed to the ‘Cutoff’ demonstrate how LFO’s can be routed to different parameters.
