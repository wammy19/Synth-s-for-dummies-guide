Envelopes.


Envelopes are often found under the name ‘Env’, ‘EG’ (Envelope Generator). Like LFO’s, envelopes are a source of modulation for parameters. However, envelopes do not generate waveforms, but rather modulation parameters in stages over a time specified by the user. The stages are usually called ‘Attack Time’, ‘Decay Time’, ‘Sustain Level’ and ‘Release Time’. This is often abbreviated to ‘A’ for attack, ‘D’ for decay, ’S’ for sustain, ‘R’ for release and in combination ’ADSR’. For the most part all synthesizers have a dedicated envelope routed to the ‘amp’ (the volume) of the synthesizer. Below is a common visual representation of the different stages an envelope undergoes.


Let’s go through the different stages as if this is routed to the amp.



Attack - Once a note is pressed, the volume will start from nothing and increase in a swell to it’s peak volume.


Decay - Once the peak is reached, the volume will decrease in a smooth swell to the specified sustain level.


Sustain - The note will carry on at the specified sustain level until the note is released.


Release - Once the note is released the volume will decrease in a long swell.
