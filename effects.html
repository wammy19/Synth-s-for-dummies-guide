Effects.


Synthesisers don’t commonly come with effects built in, but more modern synthesizers are being designed with effects included. Some common effects a synthesisers might have built in are different types of delays, reverbs, and choruses. All these effects do different things to the sound.


Reverb (short for reverberation) creates the sense of space to a sound. Delay gives an ‘echoey’ effect, and chorus gives a ‘doubling’ or ‘unison’ type of effect. Delay/Echo


‘Time’ determines the time between each repeat (echo), and feedback determines the number of repeats the echo will have.


The slider ‘Dry/Wet’ controls how loud the effected sound (wet) is against the not effected sound (dry). This is common music production jargon. This is also sometimes called ‘Mix’ or simply ‘Amount’.
